**Team Project Proposal**  
Up to 1,500 XP, due March 15

Working together in your established guild teams, you will develop a formal project proposal that addresses a business challenge encountered during our RPG scenarios. While the project itself is fictional, the proposal should demonstrate professional-quality collaborative writing and thorough understanding of proposal conventions. This assignment simulates the type of team-based proposal writing you may encounter in your future workplace.

# **Learning Objectives:**

* Practice collaborative writing strategies  
* Master proposal document conventions  
* Develop team coordination skills  
* Apply audience awareness to formal proposals  
* Demonstrate professional document design

This assignment supports the following Course Learning Outcomes:

1\. Apply rhetorical principles to analyze and adapt communication for diverse business audiences, purposes, and contexts.  
2\. Produce professional-quality documents in various business genres, demonstrating mastery of conventions and best practices.  
3\. Collaborate effectively in team environments to create, review, and revise business communications

---

# **Assignment Components:**

1. Executive Summary (200-300 words)  
* Project overview  
* Key benefits  
* Resource requirements  
* Timeline snapshot  
* Return on investment  
2. Problem Statement & Background (300-400 words)  
* Current situation analysis  
* Pain points identified from RPG scenarios  
* Organizational context  
* Stakeholder impact assessment  
* Problem significance  
3. Proposed Solution (400-500 words)  
* Detailed solution description  
* Implementation steps  
* Technical requirements  
* Success metrics  
* Innovation elements  
4. Resource Requirements (300-400 words)  
* Budget breakdown  
* Staffing needs  
* Technology requirements  
* Timeline with milestones  
* Risk mitigation resources  
5. Risk Analysis & Mitigation (300-400 words)  
* Potential challenges  
* Contingency plans  
* Alternative approaches  
* Success factors  
* Monitoring methods  
6. Collaboration Record (150-250 words per team member)  
* Each team member will draft a brief statement summarizing their contribution to the development and writing of this report.   
* Because this project is a group assignment on Canvas, only one team member will need to upload the document. Thus, this section, with summaries from each team member, needs to be included at the end of the report itself.

# **Format Requirements:**

* Professional business formatting  
* Clear headings and organization  
* Appropriate spacing and font choices  
* Citations if external sources are used

# **Team Roles & Responsibilities:**

All team members will participate equally in writing, reviewing, and revising the proposal. However, each member will also serve as the "lead" for specific aspects of the project, drawing on their academic expertise. Below are some suggested team roles, based on the majors represented in this class:

Business Administration Lead:

* Coordinates overall project vision  
* Ensures proposal aligns with organizational goals   
* Takes primary responsibility for Executive Summary  
* Leads team discussions about implementation strategy

Finance/Accounting Lead:

* Develops budget projections  
* Creates resource allocation plans  
* Takes primary responsibility for Resource Requirements section  
* Ensures financial aspects are clearly communicated

Human Resources Lead:

* Analyzes stakeholder impacts  
* Develops staffing plans  
* Takes primary responsibility for Problem Statement section  
* Ensures proposal addresses human capital needs

Project Implementation Lead:

* Develops timeline and milestones  
* Creates risk management plans  
* Takes primary responsibility for Risk Analysis section  
* Ensures practical implementation details

Note: These roles are flexible and should be adapted based on your team's specific majors and strengths. The "lead" responsibility means coordinating that section's development and ensuring its quality, but all team members will contribute to writing and revising each section.
